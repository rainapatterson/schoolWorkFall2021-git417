Let's Talk About Web Safety!

What can we find out easily?

Keep your browser software up-to-date

Newer browsers protect your computer better and make sure you are safe from any suspicious scams or viruses. Updated browsers keep your computer more secure by targetting problems that may occur, like identity theft, spyware, and any sorts of malware. This link can help you see if your current browser is up-to-date. Check your browser status.

Use HTTPS

HTTPS means Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure. You want to make sure that you see the "S" before you enter any personal information! Here is a link discussing the importance of HTTPS sites. Why HTTPS?

Don't resue passwords

A great example I read in this attached link is,

"It's as if you had multiple houses and used the same lock and key for all of them. If you were to lose your keys, all of your houses would be vulnerable."

Making sure that you change your passwords at least every 90 days can help you keep your accounts secure and ensure safety. Using different passwords can help make you less vulnerable to any vitrual theft. More on why you should never reuse the same passwords.
